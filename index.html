FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual form of money that uses cryptography for secure transactions and control of new units. It operates on blockchain technology and is decentralized, meaning it's not controlled by any central authority like a bank or government.

You can buy cryptocurrencies from cryptocurrency exchanges using fiat currency (like USD, EUR) or other cryptocurrencies. Create an account on a reputable exchange, verify your identity, and follow their instructions to make a purchase.

Blockchain is a distributed and immutable digital ledger that records transactions across multiple computers. It ensures transparency, security, and immutability in various applications beyond just cryptocurrencies.

Use hardware wallets or secure software wallets, enable two-factor authentication (2FA), and follow best practices for online security like using strong passwords and avoiding public Wi-Fi networks.

Bitcoin (BTC) was the first cryptocurrency, mainly serving as digital gold and a store of value. Ethereum (ETH) is a platform that enables developers to build decentralized applications (DApps) using its native currency, Ether.

The legal status of cryptocurrencies varies by country. Some countries have embraced them, while others have imposed restrictions or outright bans. Research the regulations in your country before getting involved.

Be cautious of unsolicited investment opportunities, verify the authenticity of projects before investing, and don't share your private keys or personal information with anyone.

Yes, some cryptocurrencies are mineable. Mining involves using computational power to validate transactions and add them to the blockchain. However, it's become more complex and resource-intensive over time.
